# GTM

The exact target user is a technical founder or VP Engineering at a 5-50 person seed or Series A startup that has adopted multiple AI tools informally: Cursor for engineers, ChatGPT Team for operators, Claude Max for founders, and API spend hidden in product experiments. They do not have procurement yet, but they do feel runway pressure and have at least one monthly AI invoice that is growing faster than headcount.

Right before they want this tool, they are likely searching or scrolling around "Cursor business worth it", "Claude Max limits", "OpenAI API bill high", "ChatGPT Team vs Plus", or posts complaining that AI coding tools got expensive. They hang out in Hacker News launch threads, r/SaaS, r/startups, r/LocalLLaMA, r/ClaudeAI, r/cursor, Indie Hackers, Latent Space Discord, AI Engineer Discord, and founder Slack groups like Hampton or On Deck alumni communities.

The first 100 users with ₹0 paid budget should come from specific wedge posts, not generic content. I would publish a teardown titled "We audited 12 Indian startup AI stacks and found the same 4 leaks" with anonymized examples, then share a calculator link in comments where people already argue about AI pricing. I would DM 50 founders who recently posted about Cursor, Claude, or OpenAI bills and offer to benchmark their stack privately. I would also give micro-audits in public replies: "4 Cursor Business seats at roughly ₹15,680/mo is fine if you need admin controls; if not, Pro is about ₹7,840/mo." Useful replies earn clicks.

Credex's unfair channel is supply-side knowledge. It sees real discounted credit availability and can say which vendors have inventory right now. That makes the tool more useful than a static pricing calculator because the CTA can be specific: "You have ₹2L/mo OpenAI retail spend; discounted credits may be available." Week-1 traction that suggests this works: 100 completed audits, 25 email captures, 8 high-savings consultation clicks, and at least 3 conversations where a founder forwards an invoice or asks about credits.
